Trojan is a 3D rail-shooter game made in Unity.

In Trojan, the player takes on the role of an anti-virus software. Its mission is to clear the computer of all the viruses that are in it. The viruses are able to fight back, but the anti-virus software upgrades itself as it progresses with new abilities that will help it achieve its mission.

Being in-charge of mostly gameplay programming gave me many opportunities to design and implement UX improvements to the game.

One such improvement was the use of particles to provide feedback to the player about the gameplay state. When enemies are hit or defeated, a unique particle effect is created. The player's weapon emit steam when overheated. Laser hits create sparks.

I aimed to provide strong feedback to the player's actions in order to instill a strong sense of satisfaction.

Information about the game is conveyed through the game world instead of interfaces.

The heat gauge is on the weapon with a needle indicating the current heat level and an image on the magazine displays the currently selected weapon.

I aimed to display whatever information I could in the game world instead of a HUD in order to provide a more immersive experience and reduce on-screen clutter.

State of combat is shown very clearly in an immersive manner.

When taking damage, a deep red vignette flashes on screen, creating an impactful feedback. The vignette is designed to allow clear vision of the centre of the screen such that it does not obstruct gameplay instead of enhancing the experience. When the player's shield is activated, the shield comes out and surrounds the player in a light blue. This bubble is translucent, allowing the player to gather information about his surroundings while staying safe.

I aimed to show combat information in a very clear fashion as it is important for smooth gameplay and player satisfaction. It is also the reason why health and shield levels are displayed via an interface instead of in-world elements.
