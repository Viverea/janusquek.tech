Trojan

Trojan is a 3D rail-shooter game developed with Unity in 2016, for my final year project during my tertiary education.

In Trojan, the player takes on the role of an anti-virus software. Its mission is to clear the computer of all the viruses that are in it. The viruses are able to fight back, but the anti-virus software upgrades itself as it progresses with new abilities that will help it achieve its mission.

Strong Player Feedback

Use of colorful particles to show feedback

Strong player feedback was implemented with the use of particles to provide feedback to the player about the gameplay state. 

When enemies are hit or defeated, a unique particle effect is created. The player's weapon emit steam when overheated. Laser hits create sparks.

Integrated UI

Heat gauge and weapon choice integrated into the weapon visually

Integrated UI was implemented with visual cues that are part of the game world and not as a HUD element.

The heat gauge is on the weapon with a needle indicating the current heat level and an image on the magazine displays the currently selected weapon.

Immediate Access to Information

Health and shield status are immediately available

Important information such as combat stats are immediately available via a HUD and impactful feedback.

When taking damage, a deep red vignette flashes on screen, creating an impactful feedback. When the player's shield is activated, the shield comes out and surrounds the player in a light blue.

Health and shield levels are shown on the HUD instead of integrated elements so that they can be immediately available.
